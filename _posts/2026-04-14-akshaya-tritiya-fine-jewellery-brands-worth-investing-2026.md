The material value of the gold or diamonds is one layer. The cultural and design value is another. And the emotional value, the piece that a daughter reaches for first when she opens her mother's jewellery box, is often the one that money cannot quantify at all.

**WHAT TO LOOK FOR BEFORE YOU BUY**

An Akshaya Tritiya investment deserves the same due diligence as any significant financial decision. The markers of a purchase worth making are consistent across categories and price points.

Hallmarking is non-negotiable for gold. BIS hallmarking in India certifies the purity of the gold you are buying, and any reputable jeweller will provide it as a matter of course. If it is not offered, ask. If it is not available, walk away.

Certification matters for diamonds. A GIA or IGI certificate for any significant diamond purchase provides independent verification of the stone's cut, colour, clarity, and carat, the four factors that determine its value. Buying a significant diamond without certification is buying a story rather than a stone.

Craftsmanship is harder to quantify but not impossible to assess. Look at the finishing on the reverse of a piece, at the quality of closures and settings, at the consistency of stones within a set. The back of a piece of jewellery tells you as much about a maker's standards as the front.

Provenance, in the fine jewellery context, means knowing who made the piece, where, and with what materials. The emerging generation of fine jewellery buyers in India is asking these questions with increasing frequency, and the brands that can answer them are the brands building the kind of trust that lasts beyond a single Akshaya Tritiya.
