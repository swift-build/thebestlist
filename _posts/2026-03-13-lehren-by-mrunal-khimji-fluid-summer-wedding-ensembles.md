Named after the Urdu word for *waves*, **Lehren** draws from the quiet rhythm and movement of the sea. The collection explores fluidity through fabrics that shift and fall with an almost weightless quality, creating silhouettes that feel effortless. Across saris, lehengas, jumpsuits and dresses, the design language blends Indian craftsmanship with subtle Arab influences, soft drapes, ornamentation and thoughtful detailing that allow the garments to breathe.

Behind the label is designer and entrepreneur **Mrunal Khimji**, whose work has consistently championed Indian craftsmanship through a contemporary lens. Her eponymous label is built on the idea that clothing should feel personal and enduring rather than trend-driven. With a focus on high quality fabrics, delicate embroidery and considered silhouettes, her collections favour pieces that move easily between seasons while retaining a sense of craft and distinction.

**Panel Lehenga with Cape Top**
Rendered in a soft coral-to-peach ombré, this panelled lehenga feels almost like a summer sunset captured in fabric. The sheer cape top glides over the silhouette, while intricate hand embroidery adds texture while uplifting the look. It’s the kind of ensemble that belongs at a breezy mehendi or a destination wedding by the water, pair it with delicate diamond studs or a light choker and let the colour do most of the talking.



![](/assets/images/posts/image-8-.png)



**Kurta with Sharara Pants and Dupatta**
A short hand-embroidered kurta paired with panelled sharara pants and a georgette dupatta scattered with sequins, this set moves beautifully with every step. The sharara creates a gentle sense of rhythm, echoing the wave-like fluidity that runs through the collection.



![](/assets/images/posts/image-9-.png)



**Lehenga Set with Cape**
In a luminous shade of yellow, this lehenga set carries the warmth of a summer afternoon. Intricate hand embroidery runs across the skirt, blouse and cape, creating a look that feels celebratory without being overly ornate. The cape adds movement and ease, perfect for a bridesmaids outfit or sunlit sangeet.



![](/assets/images/posts/image-10-.png)



**Saree with Velvet Blouse in Green**
In a deep green tone, this sari carries a richness that works beautifully for evening celebrations. The velvet blouse anchors the look, adding a sense of old-world charm against the easy drape of the fabric. It’s easy to imagine it styled with heirloom jewellery for breezy summer events.



![](/assets/images/posts/image-11-.png)



**Cape Dress**
Crafted in a striking shade of red, this cape dress brings a touch of drama to the collection. Intricate hand embroidery and feather detailing frame the silhouette, while the cape creates movement with every step. It’s a piece made for grand entrances, think cocktail nights or reception parties where bold colour and confident styling take centre stage.



![](/assets/images/posts/unnamed.png)



Guided by an inclusive philosophy of creating for “women without labels,” Mrunal continues to work closely with artisan communities, championing traditional craft and the enduring value of handmade textiles. Launching the label in India marks both a homecoming and a meaningful step forward in her journey. She views this moment as an opportunity to deepen conversations around slow fashion, conscious luxury, and the enduring relevance of craftsmanship, encouraging a greater appreciation for textile heritage while inspiring more thoughtful, lasting choices in fashion.
